How to use in a presentation

Recommended order for explaining to your teacher

1
Start with a number, not the framework. Open with: "One in three children here grows up in poverty. Utrecht average: one in eight." Then open the Bubble Map to show the full system.
2
Open the Bubble Map (01). Click Economic Equity — show how many connections come out of it. Then click Green Space. Let the visual make the argument before you explain it.
3
Open WHO 6P Criteria (02). Click Prosperity. Show the child poverty criterion: 33.6% vs the WHO benchmark of below 10%. More than three times over the limit.
4
Open the Cascade Map (03). Click Economic Equity. Watch the lines light up. Say: "This is why Marmot called it the causes of the causes."
5
Open Intervention Sequence (04). Walk through steps 1–3. Governance first, then economic equity, then green space.
6
Open Full Intervention Simulator (05). Start at baseline. Click "Greenery only" — show that green space alone is insufficient. Click "Full WHO HiAP" — show what all six pillars working together looks like.
7
Close with the sentence: "Kanaleneiland does not have a health problem. It has an equity problem that shows up as fifteen different health problems. And the evidence tells us exactly where to intervene first."
