The Meaning of Healthy Cities — Information Sources

Every source used in the visuals, maps, and analysis. Each entry explains what the study proved, why it creates a specific connection in the relationship map, and how it applies to Kanaleneiland specifically.

A Healthy City is not simply a city with good hospitals. The WHO defines it as one that continuously creates and improves its physical and social environments and expands community resources — enabling people to reach their maximum potential.

The WHO Phase VII framework (2019–2024) organises the conditions for health into six interdependent pillars — the 6Ps. No single pillar is sufficient alone. They form a causal system: economic inequality undermines housing and mental health; poor housing raises loneliness; loneliness erodes social cohesion; weak cohesion reduces resilience.

The six pillars (6P Framework) — WHO Phase VII

People
Physical health, mental wellbeing, loneliness, healthcare access — direct human health outcomes.
Place
Housing safety, transport equity, mobility — the built environment shaping daily access and activity.
Participation
Social cohesion and resilience — the fabric enabling collective coping and mutual support.
Prosperity
Economic equity and child poverty — the upstream 'causes of causes' driving every indicator.
Peace
Governance and equity — the enabling condition integrating health into all city policies.
Planet
Green space, biodiversity, air quality, climate resilience — multi-pathway environmental determinants.
